Creating a digital delivery culture

To create a digital delivery culture we must adopt new ways of working and change our behaviours.

These principles support the change we need to make together.

As DWP teams delivering digital services we will:

  1. 1. Work in the open

    We will always (when possible) work in the open. We encourage everyone to talk publicly about their work.

  2. 2. Share not send

    We share documents and work on them together to reduce e-mail traffic and increase collaborative working.

  3. 3. Meet regularly and always face to face/h1>

    Co-located teams work best. Regular face to face community meet ups work best. We will support and energise each other by working together

  4. 4. Less on process, more progress

    Focus on delivering working software rather than the process of building products.

5. Deliver quickly and iterate We will deliver small and often. We will build simple products and services which we test quickly and then iterate. We will follow agile principles. Think big, start small. 6. Start with user needs and measurable outcomes We will always start with establishing what the problem is that we are trying to solve, we will understand what our user needs are and why things are a priority. We will measure our success by focusing on outcomes. 7. Show not tell We will show not tell. 8. Don’t wait for permission Believing that you can’t cause positive change, or that you must seek permission from layers of management before giving a small improvement a go, can be stifling and lets good ideas go to waste. 9. People not resources We will invest in our people. We will ensure people are motivated, mentored, and supported so they become brilliant at their job (including opportunities for growth/career progression). 10. Build strong communities It’s not just about hiring great individuals it’s about creating great teams and building relationships across the teams creating great communities. 11. Put the user at the centre of everything we do We will ensure we identify user needs early and continue to review their needs at every stage of development.
